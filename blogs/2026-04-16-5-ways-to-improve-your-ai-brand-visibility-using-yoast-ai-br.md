<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-modern-seo-is-about-ai-visibility">Why modern SEO is about AI visibility</h2>



<p>People are no longer just searching on Google. Every day, more people are asking AI tools and Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity for recommendations. Unlike classic search engines, these tools don&#8217;t just list links; they curate answers by combining trained knowledge with information they&#8217;ve learned from the web.</p>



<p>AI platforms combine information from multiple sources to provide a single, context-aware, and custom answer. People even start treating these AI answers as personal advice, not just generic search results. This will happen more and more as search engines like Google increasingly integrate AI into their search results. As a result, the boundaries between traditional search and AI-generated answers are blurring.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="blind-spot">AI search is a blind spot for most</h2>



<p>Classic <a href="https://yoast.com/seo-tools/" id="337">SEO tools</a> track rankings, but they don’t track how your brand appears in AI answers. This leads to blind spots where your competitors might be all over the AI recommendations in your market without you realizing it.</p>



<p>What’s more, you might rank well on Google, but you could be invisible to a growing audience if AI systems ignore your brand. Your competitors can appear more often or more positively in AI recommendations. Or there&#8217;s negative sentiment in AI responses that can harm your reputation without you even knowing.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="narrative">Controlling the narrative of your brand</h2>



<p>AI platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini piece together your brand&#8217;s story from scattered sources, like reviews, news articles, social media, and your own content. If these send mixed signals, the answers an AI gives will too. That&#8217;s why you need to send a unified, consistent message. This is one of the most effective ways to reinforce your narrative across every platform.</p>



<p>Repeat your main message, whether that&#8217;s &#8220;affordable luxury&#8221; or &#8220;sustainable innovation,&#8221; everywhere, from your site content to press releases and from social media to external interviews.</p>



<p>Quickly address misinformation and respond to inaccurate reviews by publishing clarifications online. By doing this, you prevent the AI from amplifying outdated or incorrect details.</p>



<p>Support your brand&#8217;s most important attributes with structured data. Add the awards your brand won, or its unique selling points, so you can give the AI platform an all-encompassing framework to reference.</p>



<p>Remember, consistency is about repeating your most important brand aspects everywhere. Shape the narrative in such a way that the AI has no choice but to reflect the brand the way you want it to project.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="understanding-metrics">Understanding the AI visibility metrics</h2>



<p>Using the Yoast AI Brand Insights metrics helps you measure and improve your brand&#8217;s visibility in AI platforms. To make the most of it, you have to understand what metrics mean and why they matter.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">AI Visibility Index (AIVI)</h3>



<p>The AI Visibility Index (AIVI) scores (on a scale of 100) how visible your brand is on AI platforms such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. It consists of the following metrics:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Mentions</strong>, or how often your brand is cited in AI answers</li>



<li><strong>Citations</strong>, or the number of authoritative sources referencing your brand</li>



<li><strong>Sentiment</strong>, or the rate of positive vs. negative keywords associated with your brand</li>



<li><strong>Rankings</strong>, or the relative position of your brand mentions compared to your competitors</li>
</ul>



<p>The higher the AIVI score (on a scale of 0-100), the more visible your brand is in AI search results for the tracked terms. If you find that your score is low, you should focus on getting more mentions and citations. You should also work on positive sentiment around your business.</p>



<p>You build your relevance by publishing authoritative content. Try to get featured on relevant sites and monitor and improve negative sentiment around your brand. Learn more about <a href="https://yoast.com/how-ai-is-shaping-brand-perception/">how AI shapes brand perception</a>.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="The AIVI score in Yoast AI brand insights is showing a score of 62 out of 100 for a website" class="content-visible wp-image-4544770" height="357" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast-ai-brand-insights-aivi-600x357.png" width="600" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">The higher the AIVI score (on a scale of 0-100), the more visible your brand is in AI search results for the tracked terms</figcaption></figure>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Mentions</h3>



<p>The Mentions section tracks the specific queries for which your brand appears in AI responses. So, if someone asks, &#8220;What is the best low-cost CRM system for small businesses?&#8221; and your brand is in the results, that is a mention.</p>



<p>It&#8217;s not hard to understand why this is important. More mentions generally lead to greater visibility. If you don&#8217;t show up for the terms and queries relevant to your brand, you need to start improving your content.</p>



<p>Use the built-in AI-generated brand queries to find high-intent questions and write content that answers those questions thoroughly. These could be blog posts or <a href="https://yoast.com/features/structured-data-blocks/faq-block/" id="2887429">FAQ pages</a>, or whatever makes sense. Also optimize for conversational queries, such as &#8220;Is brand X good for startups?&#8221;</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Sentiment</h3>



<p>Sentiment measures the percentage of negative vs. positive words in the query results associated with your brand. So, if the AI describes your brand as &#8220;innovative&#8221; or &#8220;reliable&#8221;, that counts as positive sentiment. However, if they use terms like &#8220;overpriced&#8221; or &#8220;unreliable&#8221;, that&#8217;s negative sentiment.</p>



<p>Positive sentiment helps build trust, while negative sentiment can drive potential customers away. That&#8217;s why you should always actively address negative sentiments online. Don&#8217;t leave those bad online reviews unresponded to. You can also publish testimonials on your site to amplify positive voices, and you can do the same in your marketing messaging by talking about “a brand loved by thousands&#8221; or &#8220;award-winning&#8221; products.</p>



<p>Keep an eye on trends in your online sentiment and catch and fix issues early.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="brand and competitor sentiment in shows the sentiment surrounding your brand or a competitor's, including a percentage of how positive that sentiment is" class="content-visible wp-image-4544774" height="195" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast-ai-brand-insights-sentiment-600x195.png" width="600" /><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Sentiment measures the percentage of negative vs. positive words in the query results associated with your brand</figcaption></figure>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Citations</h3>



<p><a href="https://yoast.com/ai-citations-explained/" id="4523340">Citations</a> refer to the sources that AI platforms explicitly reference when generating an answer, not the brands mentioned within those sources. For example, if Gemini answers a query about “the best credit cards” and cites a New York Times article about best credit cards, that New York Times page is the citation. Even if the article includes brands like American Express or Chase, the citation is attributed to the publisher, not to the individual brands.</p>



<p>That said, appearing in those cited sources still matters a great deal. If your brand is consistently featured in relevant, high-authority publications like The New York Times, it increases the likelihood that AI systems will surface your brand in their responses over time. In other words, you may not receive a direct citation, but you benefit from being part of the content that AI platforms trust and rely on.</p>



<p>Over time, your brand (say, American Express or Chase) becomes more likely to be included in AI responses to queries like “best credit cards,” especially if it consistently appears in trusted sources.</p>



<p>AI platforms use citations to validate their answers. Citations from top sources, such as industry publications, enhance credibility. Find where there&#8217;s a natural match between your customers and their audience, and publish the type of content people will want to link to.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="ways-to-improve">5 Ways to improve your AI brand visibility</h2>



<p>Now that you understand the metrics, here&#8217;s how to use insights from Yoast AI Brand Insights to improve your AI visibility.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Optimize for AI citations</h3>



<p>AI platforms like Gemini, Perplexity, and ChatGPT use citations to validate their responses. So, citations increase the likelihood of your brand being included and trusted in AI-generated answers</p>



<p>Try to get featured on relevant, authoritative sites and publish guest posts on industry sites, news sites, or educational domains. Get mentioned in roundup articles, like &#8220;Top 10 tools for doing X&#8221;. Ask customers to write reviews on platforms like Capterra, G2, and Trustpilot. All of these tactics can act as proof that your brand is a well-trusted source. Remember, it must be relevant citations.</p>



<p>Make sure your content is structured so the AI can read it easily. Use clear, hierarchical headings and bullet points to make the content easy to scan. Add FAQs and publish direct answers to common questions. It is also a good idea to add <a href="https://yoast.com/features/structured-data/" id="2885554">schema markup</a> to help the AI crawlers understand your content.</p>



<p>Don&#8217;t forget to update old content regularly. The AI platforms prioritize fresh, up-to-date information when retrieving sources, so refresh your content regularly to stay relevant.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Monitor and improve brand sentiment</h3>



<p>By mentioning your brand, the AI platforms also shape how people see it. If those sentiments in the AI&#8217;s answers are negative, it can hurt your trustworthiness and cost conversions. This could signal the need for a broader reconsideration of business strategy priorities.</p>



<p>Once you find AI platforms associate your brand with negative terms (like &#8220;slow customer service&#8221;), respond to this issue publicly. For instance, you could contact customers on review sites to resolve complaints. You can also publish case studies and testimonials to steer the AI towards positive perceptions.</p>



<p>In your monitoring, you&#8217;ll also find the positive terms AI platforms associate with your brand, such as &#8220;trusted&#8221; or &#8220;innovative&#8221;. Use these terms in your marketing, in your site content, and on social media.</p>



<p>The weekly scans in Yoast AI Brand Insights track sentiment shifts for your queries over time. If sentiment drops, investigate the cause, like a recent PR issue or a product recall.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Benchmark against competitors</h3>



<p>AI visibility is also about how you compare to the competition. If they are mentioned more often or in a better light than you, they will appear more often in recommendations made by AI platforms.</p>



<p>See how your brand stacks up against competitors. Use Yoast&#8217;s Competitor ranking tab to see which brands show up a lot in AI answers. Analyze their content strategy. Do they publish more case studies? Are they active on review sites?</p>



<p>This tool shows how AI describes your brand compared to others in your market. For example, if you’re a coffee company like Taylor’s of Harrogate, you might find that Lavazza is consistently labeled as &#8220;the Italian espresso expert.&#8221; Now you know exactly what to highlight, whether it’s your heritage, roasting process, or sustainability, to stand out. Use these insights to sharpen your messaging and compete more effectively.</p>



<p>Don&#8217;t forget to check your weekly competitor analyses to see if your AI visibility is improving. Double down on the strategy that works for you. The tool also includes an historical view. This lets you look back at earlier analyses by selecting a past date, helping you compare visibility and sentiment across different points in time.</p>

<p>With the insights gathered, publish blog posts, FAQs, or landing pages and directly answer those brand-related queries. Support the content with properly structured data, such as FAQ and how-to schema, to give AI platforms more tools to understand your content.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="how-to-influence">How to influence LLMs to mention your brand</h2>



<p>Imagine this: A potential customer asks ChatGPT, &#8220;What&#8217;s the best CRM for small businesses?&#8221; If your brand isn&#8217;t mentioned in the answer, you&#8217;ve lost a customer before they even knew you existed.</p>



<p><a href="https://yoast.com/insights-across-multiple-llms-matter/">LLMs like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity</a> don&#8217;t just pull answers out of thin air. They rely on data, citations, and patterns to generate responses. If your brand isn&#8217;t part of those patterns, it’s far less likely to be mentioned, no matter how well you rank on Google.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Publish authoritative content</h3>



<p>LLMs are looking for well-structured, factually accurate content. These AI platforms love sources that provide unique insights or expert opinions, so be sure to focus on that.</p>



<p>Start with original research. Publish surveys, case studies, or industry reports with unique data. For example, &#8220;2026 State of [Your Industry] Report: Key Trends and Insights” positions your brand as an authority and gives AI platforms a reason to cite you.</p>



<p>Use the proven <a href="https://yoast.com/inverted-pyramid/" id="1477287">inverted pyramid structure</a> in your content. Start with the most important information, like key findings and conclusions, follow with supporting details, and end with background information. This makes it easier for AI to extract, digest, and use your content.</p>



<p>Don&#8217;t forget to optimize for facts. Include statistics, quotes from experts, and actionable insights. For example, instead of &#8220;Our tool is great for marketers,&#8221; say &#8220;Our tool increased conversion rates by 30% for 500+ marketers in 2025, according to our latest case study.&#8221;</p>



<p>For example, HubSpot built its authority by publishing ultimate guides, like &#8220;The Ultimate Guide to Inbound Marketing.” These guides became go-to resources for marketers, earning backlinks from industry blogs, news sites, and even competitors. As a result, HubSpot is now frequently cited in AI responses about marketing tools.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Get mentioned on relevant, high-authority sites</h3>



<p>LLMs trust reputable sources like industry publications, news sites, and review platforms. The more your brand is mentioned on these sites, the more likely it is to appear in AI responses. Please keep in mind that relevance is key here. For instance, if Yoast gets mentioned in Gardeners&#8217; World or Home and Garden, it will do little to nothing for our brand. Find the most important and relevant sources and focus on those.</p>



<p>Pitch stories to journalists or industry blogs. For example, try to get featured in &#8220;Top 10 [Your Industry] Tools in 2026&#8221; lists.</p>



<p>Encourage customers to leave reviews on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or Google Reviews. Don&#8217;t forget to respond to (negative) reviews to show engagement and transparency.</p>



<p>If possible, try to reach out to sites like HubSpot, Search Engine Journal, or industry-specific blogs and ask to write for them. Be sure to include a bio with your brand name to reinforce recognition.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Optimize for conversational queries</h3>



<p>LLMs are designed to answer natural language questions. This means you have to optimize your content for conversational queries. Conversational queries are things like &#8220;What&#8217;s the best CRM for startups?&#8221; rather than &#8220;best CRM&#8221;.</p>



<p>In your content, you should use question-focused headings. For example, answer the question &#8220;Is [Your Brand] good for small businesses?&#8221;  directly in the first paragraph to make it clear and easy to understand.</p>



<p>LLMs often answer long-tail questions, so you should target long-tail keywords. For example, instead of &#8220;project management tool,&#8221; target &#8220;best project management tool for remote teams in 2026.&#8221;</p>



<p>In support of all of this, create FAQ pages with schema markup to help AI better understand your content.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Build citations</h3>



<p>Build up a network of high-quality mentions that reinforce your brand&#8217;s authority. The more high-quality, relevant citations you have, the more likely LLMs are to mention your brand.</p>



<p>Publish assets like ultimate guides, templates, or tools that others want to reference and link to. For example, &#8220;The Ultimate Guide to [Your Industry] in 2026.&#8221;</p>



<p>Reach out to bloggers, journalists, and influencers to reference your content. For example, &#8220;We noticed you mentioned [Competitor] in your article. Here&#8217;s why [Your Brand] might be a better fit.&#8221;</p>



<p>Get featured in press releases, podcasts, or webinars. For example, &#8220;[Your Brand] Announces Groundbreaking Feature for [Industry].&#8221;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Make sure AI crawlers can reach your site</h3>



<p>It&#8217;s important to ensure that AI crawlers can discover and index your content. If your site is invisible to them for whatever reason, your brand won’t appear in AI responses.</p>



<p>Your site should be technically sound, but you can also help LLMs in other ways. Make sure your site loads fast and is mobile-friendly. Use clean URLs, good meta tags and descriptions, and alt text for images. Also, use schema on your site to help AI crawlers understand what your site is about and how it all ties together.</p>



<p><a href="https://yoast.com/features/llms-txt/">In Yoast SEO, you can activate an llms.txt file.</a> This proposed standard helps point AI crawlers to your most important content. Also, check whether your robots.txt file inadvertently blocks AI crawlers from accessing your content.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Be active in online communities</h3>



<p>LLMs are trained on and can retrieve information from forums, social media, and community platforms such as Reddit, Quora, and LinkedIn. It can improve your brand&#8217;s visibility on AI platforms if you participate there.</p>



<p>Answer questions on Quora and Reddit. Provide valuable, non-promotional answers that naturally mention your brand. For example, &#8220;As a [Your Industry] expert, I recommend [Your Brand] because…&#8221;</p>



<p>Join discussions on Slack, Discord, or niche forums. Share insights and link to your content when relevant. Post thought leadership content on LinkedIn, Twitter, or Facebook. For example, &#8220;Here&#8217;s why [Your Industry] is changing in 2026, and how [Your Brand] is leading the way.&#8221;</p>
