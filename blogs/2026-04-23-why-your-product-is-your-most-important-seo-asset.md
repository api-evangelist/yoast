<p>For a long time, we defined SEO success by rankings and traffic. If you reached the top of the search results and brought people to your site, you did your job. That approach worked when discovery was linear, and search engines were the primary gatekeepers. But modern search behavior does not stop at discovery. Users want clarity, reassurance, and confidence before they make decisions. With so many options to choose from, users want to understand what a product does, how it compares to alternatives, and whether it fits their needs.</p>



<p>There is a shift in SEO, one that pushes closer to product thinking and long-term value creation. Search engines reward content and experiences that help users make informed decisions, not just pages that match keywords. That means SEO can no longer exist solely in the acquisition channel. SEO must support the entire journey, from first touch to post-purchase experience.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-technical-seo-has-always-been-product-thinking">Technical SEO has always been product thinking</h2>



<p>Technical SEO has always mattered, and it’s been tied to product quality, or at least product page quality. Site speed, internal linking, structured content, and clear navigation all shape how users experience a product online.</p>



<p>A fast, well-structured site helps users and AI platforms better understand your products. That means better visibility in search engines and AI recommendations alike. Good SEO looks at the system as a whole, prioritizes changes based on impact, and focuses on removing friction, which are the same principles that guide good product decisions.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-think-like-a-product-marketer-not-just-an-seo">Think like a product marketer, not just an SEO</h2>



<p>Ranking for&nbsp;keywords&nbsp;does not automatically mean you are reaching the right audience or communicating the right value.&nbsp;Product marketers spend time understanding who the product is for, what problem it&nbsp;solves, and why someone should choose it over alternatives. SEO benefits enormously from that same approach.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Keyword research is not just a targeting exercise. It reveals how people describe their problems, what they care&nbsp;about, and what information they need before&nbsp;making a decision.&nbsp;Applying those insights to product descriptions, category pages, and supporting content pulls SEO closer to real user intent.&nbsp;</p>



<p>This is how SEO moves beyond traffic and starts&nbsp;contributing to the full customer journey: awareness, consideration, conversion, and, just as importantly, retention.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-your-product-is-your-most-underrated-seo-asset">Your product is your most underrated SEO asset</h2>



<p>Many SEO strategies still treat content as something separate from the product. Blogs live in one place while product pages are left to focus purely on conversion.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>But products <em>are</em> content. Product names, descriptions, specifications, FAQs, reviews, and even post-purchase information all reflect the real information users are looking for. This content often holds far more SEO value than a generic blog post. Still, most brands do not optimize it with the same level of care.</p>



<p>When product pages are clear,&nbsp;well-structured, and written in the language customers&nbsp;actually use, they become powerful discovery assets.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-ai-is-changing-how-products-are-discovered-and-bought">AI is changing how products are discovered and bought</h2>



<p><a href="https://ecommercenews.eu/58-of-consumers-use-ai-tools-to-research-products/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Users are turning to AI platforms</a>&nbsp;to&nbsp;ask&nbsp;for recommendations, evaluate&nbsp;options, and understand differences between products.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>ChatGPT now supports direct purchases through integrations with platforms like Shopify, using OpenAI’s Agentic Commerce Protocol. That means users can discover&nbsp;and buy products directly within an AI&nbsp;conversation&nbsp;without ever&nbsp;visiting&nbsp;a product page on a website.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>For businesses, this changes what visibility looks like. SEO is no longer just about ranking in search results. SEO is about making sure your products are understandable, trustworthy, and accessible to AI systems that act as intermediaries.  </p>



<p>And the scope of that is broader than it first appears. Google&#8217;s Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) extends AI-mediated commerce well beyond the checkout, covering the full lifecycle from product discovery through to order management, post-purchase support, and loyalty. That means the journey SEO needs to support has grown significantly. It is not just about being found and bought; it is about being the kind of brand an AI agent would confidently recommend, follow up with, and return to. <a href="https://www.searchenginejournal.com/agentic-commerce-what-seos-need-to-consider-acp-ucp/563503/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Read more about ACP and UCP and what they mean for SEOs.</a></p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-why-schema-matters-more-than-ever">Why schema matters more than ever</h2>



<p>If AI systems are going to recommend and sell products, they need structured information to rely on. Schema provides that structure. It tells search engines and AI&nbsp;platforms&nbsp;what a product is, how much it costs, whether it is available, how it is&nbsp;reviewed, and how it fits into a broader catalog.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Without <a href="https://yoast.com/features/structured-data/" id="https://yoast.com/features/structured-data/">structured data</a>, products become harder for machines to interpret and surface. With it, they become eligible for richer visibility across search engines,&nbsp;LLMs, and emerging shopping experiences.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>This goes beyond the basics. Pricing, availability, reviews, FAQs, shipping details, and even compatibility information all contribute to how well an AI agent can evaluate and surface your products. Third-party reviews on platforms like Trustpilot also play a role. Agents use external signals to validate brand credibility before making a recommendation. If that structured data is incomplete or inconsistent, your products risk being entirely invisible to agent-mediated discovery. </p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-conclusion">Conclusion</h2>



<p>The rules of SEO have not been torn up&nbsp;but&nbsp;extended. Product thinking, structured data, clear&nbsp;content, and technical rigor have always mattered. What has changed is the audience you are&nbsp;optimizing&nbsp;for. Alongside the human visitor, you now have AI agents evaluating,&nbsp;recommending, and, in some cases, completing purchases on a user&#8217;s behalf.&nbsp;The businesses that will thrive are those that make their products easy to understand, easy to trust, and easy to surface,&nbsp;whether a person or a machine is doing the searching.&nbsp;</p>
