<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-1-nbsp-seo-nbsp-isn-t-nbsp-dying-but-evolving-nbsp">1.&nbsp;SEO&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;dying, but evolving&nbsp;</h2>



<p><strong>51% of respondents consider SEO to be “evolving”. 33% say&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;“thriving”. Only 10% think&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;“declining”.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>This is an interesting divide, but&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;not random. In the results, those with 10+ years of experience say SEO is thriving, while newcomers say it is not. It might be that experts know the landscape better and see change as&nbsp;a&nbsp;constant.&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“SEO has always adapted to changes in the SERP, and now&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;adapting again. The traditional SERP is gone, but SEO&nbsp;isn’t.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“SEO is evolving, but not because its fundamentals are breaking. The interfaces between users and information are changing. Search is no longer confined to ten blue links, but the need for structured, relevant, trustworthy content&nbsp;hasn’t&nbsp;diminished.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>The Yoast Perspective:</strong>&nbsp;We think SEO&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;going anywhere, but there&nbsp;are changes&nbsp;happening.&nbsp;Traditional search from Google and Bing still drives&nbsp;traffic, but AI-driven&nbsp;discovery from LLM-powered assistants shapes&nbsp;perception&nbsp;and&nbsp;discovery. Therefore, the best SEOs&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;choose sides in this fight; they are mastering both directions.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="state of seo graph showing 50,9% saying evolving" class="content-visible wp-image-4545953" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_03-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="don't">2. Keep the name Search Engine Optimization </h2>



<p><strong>39% say SEO should be relabeled “Search Everywhere Optimization”. Only 32% want to keep “Search Engine Optimization”.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>Big support for relabeling SEO, and even among veterans, 41% prefer&nbsp;<em>Search Everywhere Optimization</em>. Of course, this&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;mean that we should do this.&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“The term ‘SEO’ will stay. The role will widen to include AI and other disciplines, but the name&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;need to change.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“The term ‘SEO’ still holds shared meaning, credibility, and market recognition.&nbsp;There’s&nbsp;no&nbsp;strong evidence&nbsp;that rebranding the discipline itself is necessary or beneficial. Responses favoring ‘Search Everywhere Optimization’ reflect where SEO outcomes now surface, not a fundamentally different practice.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>The Yoast Perspective:</strong>&nbsp;We at Yoast&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;think the term SEO is broken. Yes, there is a lot of change happening, especially in search, with AI overviews, chatbots, and social media platforms, but what about the core SEO work? You still&nbsp;have to&nbsp;focus on technical foundations, content quality, brand building, and authority.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>‘Search Everywhere Optimization’ might describe where SEO happens, but it doesn’t change what SEO is. The name ‘SEO’ still works, but we just need to explain how it applies to AI and social platforms.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="seo label graph showing 28.6% saying search everywhere optimization" class="content-visible wp-image-4545954" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_04-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="optimize">3. Good SEO is LLM optimization&nbsp;</h2>



<p><strong>64%&nbsp;agree&nbsp;LLM optimization is&nbsp;essentially the&nbsp;same as&nbsp;traditional SEO. 59%&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;even actively&nbsp;optimizing for&nbsp;LLMs.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>You might call this laziness, but you could also call it efficiency. It oftentimes comes down to the same thing.&nbsp;</p>



<p>There’s&nbsp;also&nbsp;the 9% who strongly disagree with this statement. These respondents say LLMs prioritize synthesis over rankings, so focusing on structured data and brand mentions makes more sense for them. Of course, they are not wrong, but they&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;contradict what others have said.&nbsp;LLMs&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;require&nbsp;new tactics; they just reward the same SEO principles more strictly.</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“If&nbsp;you’re&nbsp;undertaking good SEO,&nbsp;you’re&nbsp;already&nbsp;optimizing&nbsp;well for LLMs. The tactics&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;change—just the audience.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“The same practices that make content discoverable and trustworthy for search engines also make it usable for LLMs. The confusion arises when people treat LLMs as&nbsp;a completely separate&nbsp;system.&nbsp;In reality, LLM&nbsp;visibility rewards clarity, relevance, and authority—all long-standing SEO principles.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p>LLM optimization&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;a separate discipline because it’s&nbsp;SEO for AI. The same principles&nbsp;apply:&nbsp;clarity, structure, and authority. The difference? AI systems are less forgiving of mediocre content, so the&nbsp;bar for&nbsp;quality is higher.&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="llm optimization is the same as traditional seo graph showing 51.8% agree" class="content-visible wp-image-4545955" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_05-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="rankings">4.&nbsp;Rankings&nbsp;still matter, but not like they used to&nbsp;</h2>



<p><strong>52% say&nbsp;rankings&nbsp;are “equally important” as before. 30% say&nbsp;they’re&nbsp;“less important”.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>This is a sensible shift. Google’s AI overviews and other zero-click&nbsp;results&nbsp;mean&nbsp;visibility does not equal&nbsp;traffic. For AI systems,&nbsp;rankings&nbsp;are still an authority signal.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“Traditional rankings are still important because agents still search the web to ingest information. If you&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;visible there,&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;less likely an agent will&nbsp;identify&nbsp;and select you into their responses.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“Rankings still matter, but they are no longer the end goal. They are a proxy for visibility, not a guarantee of impact.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>The Yoast Perspective:</strong>&nbsp;We need to stop obsessing over ranking number one, so start&nbsp;tracking&nbsp;visibility and presence. Check whether you are cited in AI-driven&nbsp;answers, and&nbsp;try to be mentioned in industry&nbsp;discussions.&nbsp;AI&nbsp;visibility and&nbsp;citations&nbsp;are the new&nbsp;rankings.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="how important are rankings as a kpi in 2026 graph showing 51.9% saying equally important" class="content-visible wp-image-4545956" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_11-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="organic">5. Organic&nbsp;traffic is still king, but for how long?&nbsp;</h2>



<p><strong>55% say “organic&nbsp;traffic” is their top metric. Yet 49% cite “reducing organic clicks” as their biggest challenge.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>We see this as the great paradox of 2026.&nbsp;Traffic is down, but the value of that&nbsp;traffic could be up. You might get less&nbsp;traffic, but the clicks that do happen have a better intent.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong><em>&nbsp;“As AI reduces the need for some visits, success looks like being represented correctly rather than merely visited. Visibility in AI overviews doesn’t always drive clicks, but it builds legitimacy. Being included signals that you’re a credible source, even when users don’t click.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>Our advice:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Work on AI visibility, as this is the new SEO metric. Just as rankings show your visibility in traditional search, citations in AI overviews show your authority in AI-driven discovery. Track it alongside rankings and traffic&nbsp;</li>



<li>Keep an eye on branded search volume to learn whether people are looking for you by name&nbsp;</li>



<li>Monitor citations to see if others are referencing your content online&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="what are the most important seo metrics in 2026 graph showing 54.5% choose organic traffic" class="content-visible wp-image-4545957" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_09-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="content">6. Content saturation is a big threat </h2>



<p><strong>39% say “competing with AI-generated content” is their top challenge. Only 4% cite a “talent gap.”</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>We know AI can write bad content. But&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;a bigger challenge when AI writes good enough content&nbsp;at&nbsp;scale. This will flood the web with noise, making it hard to penetrate.&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“AI-generated content is artificial. Humans connect with stories, not regurgitated lists.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“AI&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;change what&nbsp;good content&nbsp;is, but just raises the bar. Mediocrity&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;just rank lower; it disappears.”</em>&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>Our advice:</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Focus on building your EEAT, because AI&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;fake real-world&nbsp;expertise&nbsp;and authority&nbsp;</li>



<li>Prioritize quality over quantity, as a single great piece of content can beat ten average ones&nbsp;</li>



<li>Use AI, but be careful and always use it as a tool, not as a replacement&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="biggest challenges in seo in 2026 graph showing 49% choose reducing organic clicks" class="content-visible wp-image-4546225" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_2026_q_19-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="ignore">7. Most SEOs are ignoring a fast-growing search channel&nbsp;</h2>



<p><strong>Traditional search (Google/Bing) is still #1. But&nbsp;TikTok&nbsp;search&nbsp;ranks #5, lower than Amazon.</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>This might be something of a blind spot for many. Younger generations use TikTok and other video platforms for entertainment, recommendations, tutorials, and even&nbsp;B2B&nbsp;advice.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Alex Moss’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“Social platforms influence how LLMs perceive freshness and authority. Ignoring them means&nbsp;missing out on&nbsp;signals that AI systems value.”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p><strong>Carolyn Shelby’s take:</strong>&nbsp;<em>“You&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;need to rank on TikTok, but you do need to be discoverable there. LLMs scrape social platforms for real-world signals.”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<p><strong>The Yoast Perspective:</strong>&nbsp;SEO now includes social platforms like TikTok. You don’t need to rank there, but you do need to be discoverable, because LLMs scrape these platforms for fresh, authoritative content.&nbsp;A great video&nbsp;channel can boost your authority in AI responses.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Our advice:</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Repurpose content for video platforms like TikTok and YouTube&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>



<li>Check brand mentions in these platforms&nbsp;</li>



<li>Improve your video SEO in general&nbsp;</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><img alt="which search channels are you prioritizing most in 2026 graph showing traditional search engines at number one" class="content-visible wp-image-4552396" height="338" src="https://yoast.com/app/uploads/2026/04/yoast_perspective_q_16-600x338.jpg" width="600" /></figure>
